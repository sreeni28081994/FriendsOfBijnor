    History

    Bijnor Mission, the diocese of Bijnor is a first-class miracle in the modern world evangelization. It started from scratch. Missionaries came with empty hands. As in the Apostles they came to a strange land. There was nothing that could be said they owned. No home no land; strange people; strange culture; strange climate; strange geographical conditions. They came into the Holy land of the Hindus. The high peaks of the Himalayan Mountain were supposed to be the abode of Gods and Goddesses. The holy sages of the old lived in the beautiful and serene surroundings of the Himalayan Mountains. One could easily imagine the plight of the Christian missionaries coming into such a holy land.

    The missionaries worked and struggled hard wondering for more than two weeks to find a house on rent to start with. People wouldn’t trust them, strangers as they were. A section of the people welcomed the arrival of the missionaries who would open schools for the better education of their children. Many had misgiving. These missionaries would convert. Many of our people may become Christians. Our holy places will be desecrated. And the curse of God will come upon us. They will bring foreigners to conquer and possess our land. They will rule our people. But soon the people realized the missionaries came to their service.

    They saw the missionaries engaging themselves in various fields of services. Field of education; field of health care; field of socio-developmental activities. Their antagonism slowly began to die out. People welcomed the missionaries, trusted them. They invited to survey places to open a school, to open a health center, to start social work. Gradually the missionaries moved out from the towns to interior villages. They began to give more and more attention to the poor and the marginalized sections of the population. Opened several centers of non-formal education, adult education, and community health. Started schemes to provide drinking water, better health facilities, better education. They picked up leaders from the villagers, trained them various ways, made them work in their own villages for their own growth and development, look after hygiene environment and control of contagious diseases. Organized groups – self-help groups; mahila mandals (Women’s groups); youth organizations. They taught them that development won’t come from outside, it must grow from among themselves.

    People forgot their antagonism, became very friendly with missionaries, and looked forward to them for leadership and guidance. People cooperated the result was tremendous and was appreciated by all. There is evidence in this area among the people a sense of responsibility for their own growth and development. The tremendous change of attitude among the people regarding education of their own children towards their responsibility for their own development, towards health care, realization of the power and effectiveness of coming together, cooperation, united effort, self-organization etc. though have only begun to show themselves, are very powerful evidence of the fruitfulness of the work we are doing in the various fields.

    The Lord was with the missionaries as He was with the Israelites, the apostles. He worked miracles for them. Empty handed they came. God gave them friends and supporters through whom support, and timely assistance arrived. They were able to initiate big projects. Instances of God’s direct intervention among the missionaries are many and varied. In procuring land, in securing friends, in finding drinking water, in ensuring cooperation of the people. Each one is a miracle in the way it happened. To their own surprise the missionaries found many things happened. They thanked God united in mind and heart. They worked under the protection of the Lord with the kind, gracious and generous support. The result is what we see today in the diocese.

    The church must grow from the people. Sincere attempts are made for sincere inculturation to get the church sprout and grow in this soil, among this people, in their culture, in their life situation. We have full faith and great hope in our lord that he will bless our work to bear fruits and it yields.

    Thank you, Lord!

    We thank our friends, our supporters, our well-wishers... God bless us all.
